Discover The Beautiful World

Udaipur

Day 1


City Palace

Next, immerse yourself in the grandeur of Udaipur at the City Palace, the largest royal complex in Rajasthan. Built in 1559 by Maharana Uday Singh, it served as the center of power for the Maharanas. Marvel at the palace's intricate architecture, blending medieval, European, and Chinese influences. Explore its courtyards, pavilions, and corridors, and don't forget to visit the museum showcasing Rajput art and culture.


Lake Pichola

Begin your soulful journey in the heart of Udaipur with a visit to the serene Lake Pichola. This ancient, artificial lake is not just a body of water; it's a living canvas of tranquility and beauty. Encircled by majestic hills, historic buildings, and bathing ghats, Lake Pichola is a sanctuary for peace and nature enthusiasts. As the sun rises, embark on a magical boat ride, watching the heritage buildings shimmer in the golden hues reflected on the pristine waters. Lake Pichola is a masterpiece crafted by Pichhu Banjara in 1362 AD, and it has witnessed the grandeur of Udaipur's history. Admire the lush green hills, the regal City Palace on the eastern banks, and the enchanting Lake Palace resting in the middle.



Jagdish Temple

Our journey takes us to the historic Jagdish Temple, built in 1651 during Maharana Jagat Singh's reign. This masterpiece of Maru-Gujaru architecture houses Lord Vishnu and is a testament to Mewar's resilience against Mughal invaders. Legend has it that the temple's marble slab possesses mystical healing powers. Experience the spirituality and history of this sacred site.


Day 2


Bagore ki Haveli

Start your day with a visit to Bagore ki Haveli, an opulent palace built in the 18th century on the shores of Lake Pichola. Explore its hundred-plus rooms adorned with mesmerizing mirror and glass work. The Queenâ€™s Chamber features exquisite glass and mirror sculptures of peacocks. Today, the haveli has been transformed into a museum, where you can delve into history, culture, and enjoy the enchanting Dharohar Dance Show every evening.



Saheliyon ki Bari

Nature beckons as we explore Saheliyon ki Bari, the Garden of Maidens. This rare marvel was built exclusively for women in the Mewar Kingdom, a testament to the region's unique history. Stroll through the lush garden, marvel at marble pavilions, kiosks in the pool, the lotus pond, and rain fountains. Feel the wonder of nature's creation.


Sajjangarh Palace

Our journey continues to Sajjangarh Palace, also known as the Monsoon Palace. Perched atop the Aravalli hill, this palace offers breathtaking views of Lake Fateh Sagar, City Palace, and Chittorgarh. Built in 1884, it is a treasure trove of Mewar's history and culture. Let the stories of the palace and its residents transport you through time.


Day 3


Fatehsagar Lake

Start your day by embracing the stillness of Fatehsagar Lake, a mirror reflecting the tranquility of nature. The ripples on its crystal-clear surface offer a glimpse into your soul. Udaipur, the City of Lakes, celebrates nature's gifts, and Fatehsagar Lake embodies its serene beauty.



Shilpgram

Discover the rural arts and crafts of Western India at Shilpgram, an ethnographic museum complex spread over 70 acres. Explore traditional huts showcasing the lifestyles of tribal communities. This museum serves as a bridge to connect you with the rich culture and heritage of Rajasthan's tribal people.


Bahubali Hills

Conclude your journey with a visit to Bahubali Hills, a rocky viewpoint overlooking Badi Lake and the Aravali Hills. Escape the city's hustle and bustle and immerse yourself in the breathtaking vistas of Udaipur's natural beauty. These hills offer a perfect perspective to appreciate the majesty of the "Lake City."
